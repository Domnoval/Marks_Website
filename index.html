Opening

Lionsgate was not created simply as a venue. It was designed as an environment where conversations become clearer, decisions become more grounded, and people leave with a sense of direction.

My Role

I work with individuals and small groups to facilitate conversations that matter — whether strategic, personal, or creative.

This can include leadership discussions, planning sessions, or simply creating the conditions for meaningful dialogue. The space supports the process, but the intention behind it is what shapes the outcome.

What This Looks Like

  • Facilitating structured or open-ended discussions
  • Helping clarify priorities and direction
  • Supporting strategic planning and decision-making
  • Creating an environment where ideas can develop without distraction
  • Bridging perspectives across participants

Background

My professional background spans corporate strategy, global leadership, and entrepreneurial ventures. I have held senior roles at organizations such as Novartis, leading strategic planning and cross-functional initiatives, and have worked extensively in business development, operations, and organizational design.

I hold an MBA from Harvard and a BA from Columbia University, where I graduated summa cum laude and Phi Beta Kappa.

In parallel, I have developed and curated physical environments designed to support reflection, creativity, and connection.

Why Lionsgate

Lionsgate was built to support this kind of work. The layout, the transitions between spaces, the integration of art and landscape — all of it is intentional. It creates a setting where people can step outside of routine and engage more fully with what matters.

Ways to Engage

  • Venue-only use for private gatherings or events
  • Facilitated sessions for individuals or small groups
  • Combined experiences integrating space, structure, and guidance

Each engagement is different.
The common thread is simple: creating the conditions for something meaningful to happen.
